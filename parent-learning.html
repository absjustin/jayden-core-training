Week 3-4 Propulsion Fundamentals

Key Concepts to Learn

  • "Anchoring" the hand vs. "slipping" water

Stroke Mechanics Deep Dive

Understanding the technical details of all four strokes

Week 5-6 Freestyle & Backstroke

Freestyle Key Points

  • Body rotation: 45-60 degrees each side
  • Timing: catch begins as opposite arm exits
  • Kick: 6-beat vs 2-beat, hip-initiated
  • Breathing: rotation not lifting, one goggle in water
  • Hand entry: shoulder-width, fingertips first

Backstroke Key Points

  • Rotation: shoulders 45+¬∞, hips ~30¬∞
  • Entry: pinky first, just outside shoulder
  • Pull: bent arm at 90¬∞ at deepest point
  • Exit: thumb leads, quick recovery
  • Head: completely still, water line at ears
Week 7-8 Breaststroke & Butterfly

Breaststroke Key Points

  • Timing: pull ‚Üí breathe ‚Üí kick ‚Üí glide (never simultaneous)
  • Pull: "heart shape" outsweep then insweep
  • Kick: heels to butt (not knees to chest), feet turn out
  • Glide: full streamline, 2-3 count minimum

Butterfly Key Points

  • Undulation: initiated from chest press, not head bob
  • Timing: "big kick" on entry, "small kick" on exit
  • Arms: wide entry, keyhole pull, exit past hips
  • Breathing: chin forward (not up)

Age-Group Training Science

What works (and doesn't) for young swimmers

Week 9-10 Long-Term Athlete Development

LTAD Stages

  • FUNdamentals (ages 6-8): Basic movement, play
  • Learn to Train (ages 8-11): Jayden is HERE
  • Train to Train (ages 11-15): Building aerobic base
  • Train to Compete (ages 15-18): Race-specific training

Critical Principles for 10 & Under

  • Technique before fitness - Neural pathways are forming
  • All four strokes - Builds complete swimmer, prevents overuse
  • Multi-sport exposure - Helps, not hurts, swimming
  • Avoid overtraining - Young bodies recover differently
  • Keep it fun - Burnout is the biggest threat
Reality Check

Elite 10&U times are NOT predictive of senior success. Many Olympians were average at 10, great at 16+. The goal now: fall in love with swimming, build skills.

Week 15-16 Video Analysis Basics

Why Video is Essential

  • Swimmers can't feel what they're doing wrong
  • "I thought I was doing it!" is almost always true
  • Immediate visual feedback accelerates learning 3-5x

Equipment Needed

  • Smartphone in waterproof pouch (~$15)
  • Goggles strap camera (optional, ~$40)
  • Slow-motion capability (most modern phones)

Starts, Turns & Finishes

The "free speed" that wins races

Week 17-18 Racing Starts

Components of a Great Start

  • Stance: toes over edge, weight forward
  • Reaction: push with legs at the signal
  • Drive: explosive hip extension
  • Entry: streamline position, single entry hole
  • Underwater: dolphin kicks, breakout before 15m
Week 19-20 Turns & Finishes

Flip Turn Key Points

  • Approach: last stroke at flags, accelerate into wall
  • Somersault: tuck tight, chin to chest
  • Push-off: feet shoulder width, 45 degrees
  • Streamline: tight, underwater dolphins, breakout past flags

Finishes (Worth 0.1-0.3 seconds!)

  • Never glide in‚Äîaccelerate through the touch
  • Free/Back: head down, drive to wall
  • Breast/Fly: lunge, extend arms fully

Putting It All Together

Integration and ongoing development

Week 21-22 Practice Planning

Session Structure

  • Warm-up (15-20%): easy swimming, activate muscles
  • Main set (60-70%): skill work, drill, some intensity
  • Speed/power (10-15%): short, fast, full recovery
  • Cool-down (10%): easy, light stretching

Working with Team Coaches

  • Your role: supplemental, not contradictory
  • Ask: "What should we focus on at home?"
  • Share: what you're observing, not what you're correcting
  • Respect: their expertise and relationship with Jayden
